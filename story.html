Section 03 — La Storia

A bit of
Italy.

How Marco & Sofia Castelli turned a brick storefront on East Berry into Fort Wayne's heraldic Italian café — the first to pull Caffè Vergnano in Indiana, and a small fortezza in the heart of historic Ke-Ki On-Ga.

001 — The Founders

Marco & Sofia Castelli.

Marco grew up in Turin, where coffee isn't a transaction — it's the punctuation between every part of the day. Sofia, raised between Naples and Bologna, came of age in pastry kitchens. Together they decided Fort Wayne deserved a real Italian café — something with weight.

They named it Fortezza — fortress — because the Castelli family has always built places that hold. When you walk in, one of them is at the counter. They'll know your face by the second visit. By the third, your drink. By the fifth, your kid's name. That's the entire idea.

A Place Name

Why "Ke-Ki On-Ga"?

Long before it was Fort Wayne, the Miami people called this place Ke-Ki On-Ga — the village at the meeting of three rivers. It was a center of trade, defense, and welcome. A natural fortress.

We named our café Fortezza for the same reason — and we honor the older name on every cup, every menu, every door. Ke-Ki On-Ga is the soul of the place we get to call home.

002 — How We Got Here

From Turin.
To East Berry.

— Chapter One —

Italy.

Marco's family ran an espresso bar in the Piedmont — the same neighborhood where Caffè Vergnano was founded in 1882. He learned to pull a shot at twelve, and never stopped. Sofia trained as a pastry chef in Naples, where dough is folded the way her grandmothers taught.

— Chapter Two —

America.

They moved to the Midwest, raised two boys, ran restaurant kitchens. Every drip-machine cup of coffee was a small disappointment. They started talking about how it should be, and one day stopped just talking.

— Chapter Three —

The Beans.

They went hunting for the right roaster. Caffè Vergnano — the same Turin family that had filled Marco's nonno's cup since 1882 — agreed to ship to Indiana. Fortezza became the first café in Fort Wayne to pour it.

— Chapter Four —

The Fortress.

They found a brick storefront on East Berry — heart of downtown Fort Wayne, walking distance from the Old Fort. Painted it warm, hung the cavaliere on horseback above the door, fired up a lever espresso machine, and opened. The line on the second day told them they'd done it right.

003 — How We Run It

Six things
we don't compromise on.

/01

Real espresso.

Pulled fresh, every cup. No automatic super-machines. No pre-ground hoppers sitting all morning.

/02

Real ingredients.

Whole milk that tastes like milk. Italian flour. Sicilian pistachios. Things you can pronounce.

/03

Real time.

A pour-over takes five minutes. A cannolo is filled to order. We won't rush the things that matter.

/04

Real welcome.

We greet every person who walks in. By name when we know it. By eye contact when we don't.

/05

Local ties.

Pastries from a Naples-trained baker downtown. Granola from a Fort Wayne kitchen. Honey from down the road.

/06

Reasonable prices.

A great cappuccino shouldn't cost $9. We price like Italians — for everyday, not for tourists.
